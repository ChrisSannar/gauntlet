# ADR 0015: Calendar and weekly rhythm

## Status
Accepted (2026-07-06)

## Context
Chris needs a few days of setup before starting and doesn't want weeks tied to a
weekday. He committed to 60 hrs/week and wants time for cooking, chores, networking,
and his existing `/daily` practice.

## Decision
- **Kickoff**: Chris sends a message the day before a week starts. A week = 7 calendar
  days from its start, **deadline at midnight on day 7**.
- **Rhythm**: 6 days × 10 hours, one day off per week (his choice of day).
- **Blog cadence**: a post publishes when he moves on to the next project, sourced
  from the graded personal critique (ADR 0013). The blog itself is not a pass
  requirement, but the critique is.
- **Reset survivability**: anything already published (blog posts, X posts) stays up
  as public record — including the failure post. *(Design call made at Chris's
  delegation: published record is the point of public stakes.)*
- **Reading budget**: up to ~4–5 hrs/week of assigned learning-science/engineering
  reading, applied only where the week's build needs it, and graded only through how
  it shows up in the build.

## Consequences
- SPECs and rubrics reference "day N" of a week, never weekday names.
- The Teacher's daily X posts continue on the day off (automated by then).
